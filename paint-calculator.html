How to Calculate Paint Needed

Painting is one of the most cost-effective ways to refresh a room. But buying too little paint means an extra trip to the store, and buying too much is a waste of money.

The basic formula: Calculate the total wall area (perimeter × height), subtract doors and windows, then divide by the paint's coverage rate (usually 350–400 sq ft per gallon).

Paint Coverage Guide

  • Standard paint: Covers ~350 sq ft per gallon
  • Primer: Covers ~300 sq ft per gallon
  • Premium paint: Covers ~400 sq ft per gallon
  • Always buy 10% extra to account for touch-ups and spillage

Tips for a Perfect Paint Job

  • Use 2 coats for best coverage, especially for dramatic color changes
  • Prime bare drywall or bare wood before painting
  • Don't forget the ceiling — it's often the largest surface in the room
  • Measure twice, buy once! Use our calculator before heading to the store
