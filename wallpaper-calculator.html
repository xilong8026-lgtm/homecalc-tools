How to Calculate Wallpaper Needed

Wallpaper calculation is more complex than paint because patterns must match at the seams. A 12" pattern repeat means each strip may waste up to 12" of paper.

The formula: (Room Perimeter × Wall Height − Openings) ÷ Roll Coverage × Pattern Waste Factor = Rolls Needed.

Pattern Repeat Guide

  • Straight Match (0" repeat): No waste for pattern — most efficient
  • 6" Repeat: Minimal waste, ~10% extra needed
  • 12" Repeat (Standard): ~15% extra needed (most common)
  • 18–24" Repeat: Large patterns, ~20–25% extra needed

Standard Roll Sizes

Standard (US): 21" × 33 ft = 56 sq ft per roll. Usually covers ~30–35 sq ft of wall (due to matching waste).

European: 20.5" × 33 ft = 36 sq ft per roll. Covers ~20–25 sq ft of wall.

Double Roll: 27" × 33 ft = 72 sq ft. Covers ~40–50 sq ft of wall.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How much wallpaper do I need for a 10×10 room?

A 10×10 ft room (400 sq ft wall area) needs approximately 4-5 double rolls (European size) or 8-10 single rolls (US size). Our calculator gives exact estimates based on your wall dimensions.

What does pattern repeat mean?

Pattern repeat is the vertical distance before the wallpaper design repeats. Larger repeats (18-24″) need more paper because you must match the pattern at seams. Our calculator automatically adds more rolls for larger repeats.

Should I include doors and windows?

Yes, check "Include doors & windows" in our calculator. It subtracts their area from total wall area. However, add 1 extra roll for complex rooms with many openings.

How many rolls should I buy extra?

Buy 1-2 extra rolls (keep them unopened). Wallpaper batches can vary slightly in color. If you need more later, the new batch might not match perfectly.

Can I wallpaper over existing wallpaper?

Not recommended. Old wallpaper may bubble, peel, or show seams through new paper. Strip old paper first, then prime walls for best results. Some vinyl wallpapers can be painted over instead.
