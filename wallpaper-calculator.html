How to Calculate Wallpaper Needed

Wallpaper calculation is more complex than paint because patterns must match at the seams. A 12" pattern repeat means each strip may waste up to 12" of paper.

The formula: (Room Perimeter × Wall Height − Openings) ÷ Roll Coverage × Pattern Waste Factor = Rolls Needed.

Pattern Repeat Guide

  • Straight Match (0" repeat): No waste for pattern — most efficient
  • 6" Repeat: Minimal waste, ~10% extra needed
  • 12" Repeat (Standard): ~15% extra needed (most common)
  • 18–24" Repeat: Large patterns, ~20–25% extra needed

Standard Roll Sizes

Standard (US): 21" × 33 ft = 56 sq ft per roll. Usually covers ~30–35 sq ft of wall (due to matching waste).

European: 20.5" × 33 ft = 36 sq ft per roll. Covers ~20–25 sq ft of wall.

Double Roll: 27" × 33 ft = 72 sq ft. Covers ~40–50 sq ft of wall.
